3DEXPERIENCE Platform Novelties
Technical Article

Abstract

The 3DEXPERIENCE platform provides a unique visual signature as well as a clear and unified access to key services for all its apps. It also applies a brand new graphic charter that is fully compliant with touch devices.

Concerning the CAA user experience platform, the 3DEXPERIENCE platform changes the way to integrate and access interactive commands. This article gives you an overview of the user interface novelties.

The Action Bar

A New Concept

The action bar presents a more unified user experience across apps (formerly called workbenches), where all toolbars are contained in one single bar displayed at the bottom of the application frame.

The design of the action bar has been guided by the following principles:

  • Action bar :

    It contains app commands.

  • A clear organization :

    Commands are organized by sections, so that users only see what is relevant to the current context.

  • Progressive disclosure :

    The initial appearance can be expanded or customized to gradually introduce complexity.

  • Multi-platform consistency :

    The action bar supports both touch and mouse/keyboard interactions.

Action Bar Characteristics

The action bar will become the new location of most of the commands in your former CAA toolbars. This section presents the different items of the action bar.

  • A Fixed Area:

    This area is always displayed. It houses standard commands that are frequently needed and that don't depend on the data model or active app.

  • Sections:

    Sections represent a new way to group commands in the action bar. One section is visible at the time and can be changed by clicking on another section button.

  • Primary Commands:

    They are the most frequently used commands in a section, and are always visible.

  • Secondary Commands:

    They are commands that aren't frequently used. They are hidden by default and can be accessed by clicking on a an arrow on the right hand side of the action bar.

  • Flyouts:

    They are used to combine very similar commands.

We will go further into the details of the action bar and sections in the following article "3DEXPERIENCE Platform Adoption Tips"[1].

In Short

The 3DEXPERIENCE platform was delivered as from R2014. The impact for CAA applications developed before then is mainly the location of commands in the frame, due to the suppression of toolbars and the menu bar.

CAA workbenches will need to be declared as apps in a service called MyApps[3]. Once your CAA apps are declared, they become visible in the compass and can be launched. Add-ins remain accessible. By default, your old toolbars appear underneath the action bar and a special button in the action bar gives you access to the former menu bar if you had commands stored in menus. It's possible to interactively add your CAA commands in the action bar, via session options customization in the "Me services" menu of the top bar.

However, it is important to migrate your commands to adopt the 3DEXPERIENCE platform signature principles and make them accessible from the most adequate location in the frame.

The technical article "3DEXPERIENCE Platform Adoption Tips" will give you all the necessary recommendations [1] . An example of use case migration is described in the article "Migration of a CAA Geometry sample" [2].
