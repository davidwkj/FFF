Creating Elongated Holes

  1. From the Sketch section of the action bar, click Cylindrical Elongated Hole .

    Tools Palette displays boxes for defining the cylindrical elongated hole.

  2. Enter the coordinates for the circle's center in Tools Paletteand press Enter.

    The center is used to create both the big radius (radius and angle of the cylindrical elongated hole) and the small radius (circular extremities used to define the cylindrical elongated hole).

  3. Enter the coordinates for the arc's start point in the and press Enter.

    The arc appears as a construction arc.

    Note: You can also specify the arc's big radius and angle.

  4. Enter the coordinates for the arc's end point and press Enter.

    The arc appears as a construction element.

    Note: At this step, you cannot define the arc's big radius and angle.

  5. Enter the coordinates for the point on cylindrical elongated hole and press Enter.

    In other words, you are defining what we call the small radius. This small radius corresponds to the width of the cylindrical elongated hole, relatively to the circle center.
