Connecting Curves

You can connect two elements using splines and arcs.

A connecting curve (spline) is associative, and it can be continuous in point, in curvature or in tangency with its support curves. You can define the tension value and the direction of the continuity at each connecting point, as well as add constraints to the connecting curve. Moving a connecting curve changes the shape of the support curves accordingly.

This task shows you how to:

  • Connect with an Arc
  • Connect with a Spline

Connect with an Arc

You can connect two elements using an arc.

Before you begin:
  • Sketch three intersecting lines.
  • Make sure that the Open dialog boxes with legacy appearance check box available at Me > Preferences > All Preferenes > 3D Modelling > 3D Modeling Core > Sketcher > Dialogs appearance, is selected.

  1. From the Sketch section of the action bar, click Connect .

    The connect options appear in the Tools Palette.

    By default, Connect with a Spline is active and its related options are displayed.

  2. Click Connect with an Arc .

    The Connect with a Spline option is no longer available in the Tools Palette.

  3. Select the first element to connect (starting point), and then the second element (ending point).



    Tip: The point on which you click to select the first and the second element is important. The closest point to where you click is used as the starting point and the end point of the connecting curve. Always click close to the point you want to connect, or click the point itself. A connecting arc appears, tangent to both selected elements.



    Important: You cannot trim nor break conic curves. To workaround this, you can project the conic curve of interest onto another sketch, then trim the projected elements.

Connect with a Spline

You can connect two elements, using a connecting curve (a spline) that goes through their end points.

Before you begin: Sketch two splines.

  1. From the Sketch section of the action bar, click Connect .

    Tools Palette displays connection and continuity options for defining the connection.

  2. Select Continuity in tangency as the connection type you want to perform.

    By default,Continuity in tangency is the default connection type.

  3. Select both splines you want to connect to each other.



    Tip: Locations where you click to select the first and the second element are important. The closest point to where you click is automatically used as the starting point and the end point of the connecting curve. Always click close to the point you want to connect, or click the point itself.

    A connecting spline appears: it is continuous in curvature to both selected elements. Both splines are connected.

  4. Right-click the connecting curve and select xxx.object > Isolate to trim it, split it, or break it.
