Visualizing Broken Constraints between 2D and Generated Elements

You can visualize broken constraints, following the modification or deletion of generated geometry.

Broken constraints can be of the following types: parallelism, perpendicular, coincidence, concentric, tangency and symmetry.

Before you begin: Open a 2D shape and open or create the associated drawing representation.
  • From the View section of the action bar, select Show Constraints.
  • Or select the Display constraints check box from Me > Preferences > All Preferences > 3D Modeling > Mechanical Systems > Drafting > Geometry section, under Constraints Display.
In the drawing, add some 2D geometry in the drawing and create constraints between this 2D geometry and generated elements.
Related Topics
About Constraints

  1. Modify the 2D shape in such a way that some constrained generated geometry will be modified or deleted.

  2. From the standard area of the action bar, click Update.

    Broken constraints are represented by a red warning-like symbol . Valid constraints are also displayed.

    Notes:

    • When deleting a use-edge, the constraint created with it is broken.
    • When selecting the geometry and its constraints for copy-paste operations, broken constraints are kept and pasted too. If the entire geometry is not selected, constraints are not copied.
    • The color used to visualize broken constraints is the color defined for inconsistent elements by selecting Activate geometries' diagnostic check box and clicking Configure in Me > Preferences > All Preferences > 3D Modeling > Mechanical Systems > Drafting > Display section, under Elements Analysis.
    • The size used as a reference to display broken constraints is the same as the reference size defined for constraints (2 mm by default) in Me > Preferences > All Preferences > 3D Modeling > Mechanical Systems > Drafting > Geometry section, under Constraints Display.

  3. Move the pointer over the broken constraint symbol, to find out the original type of a broken constraint.

    The constraint type is displayed in the status bar.

  4. Right-click a broken constraint and select Delete or press the Del key.

    The broken constraint is deleted.

  5. Optional: Delete some geometry.

    The associated constraint is also deleted.

You have now learn to visualize broken constraints.
