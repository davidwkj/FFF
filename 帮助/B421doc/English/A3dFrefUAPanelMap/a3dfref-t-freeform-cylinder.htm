Creating a Freeform Cylinder

You can create a component based on the shape of a cylinder, then customize that shape into a unique design.

  1. From the Freeform section of the action bar, click Cylinder .

    A cylinder is displayed in the work area.

  2. Select a plane or planar face for the freeform.

    Note: Repeatedly press q to cycle through the orientation of the freeform on the various planes. Pressing m toggles the freeform in and out of the mid-plane position.

  3. Click in the work area to place the cylinder in the approximate location you want.

  4. Optional: From the work area, click the following command options in the Freeform Cylinder dialog box to adjust end conditions of the feature:

    Option Description
    Midplane. Extends the feature equally in both directions from the sketch plane. Specify overall length.
    Flip. Reverses the orientation of the feature and direction of the pull.

  5. Click in the text boxes and enter the number of subdivision in the mesh cage.



  6. Drag the arrows to define the height and width.

  7. Click in Freeform Cylinder dialog box.

  8. Drag the Robot arrows to position the cylinder.



  9. Optional: Drag the mesh points and faces to customize the shape of the cylinder.

    To assist with selecting the mesh entities, the following options are available.

    • Press o to select the faces around the freeform. Click near an edge of the face, to select the direction of the loop.
    • Press i to select the ring around the freeform.

    Option Description
    Selects any mesh entities.
    Selects vertices.
    Selects edges.
    Selects faces.
    Selects the ring edges of a loop.
    Selects the edge of a loop.
    Selects the faces of a loop.
    Filters out the freeform mesh items in back, not visible in the display.
    Displays the mesh cage.
    Sets the transparency of the freeform.
